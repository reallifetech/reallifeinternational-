World War II – The Deadliest Conflict in History

World War II erupted in 1939 when Nazi Germany, led by Adolf Hitler, invaded Poland. This led Britain and France to declare war on Germany. The Axis Powers—Germany, Italy, and Japan—fought against the Allies—Britain, France, Soviet Union, China, and later the United States.

The war involved massive battles, including the Blitzkrieg in Europe, the Eastern Front, and campaigns in North Africa and the Pacific. Civilian populations suffered enormously, including the Holocaust, where millions of Jews and minorities were systematically exterminated.

The United States entered the war after Japan attacked Pearl Harbor in 1941. D-Day in 1944 marked the beginning of the end for Nazi Germany. In 1945, Germany surrendered, and shortly after, atomic bombs forced Japan’s surrender.

Key Outcomes

  • Over 70 million people died worldwide.
  • Creation of the United Nations to promote international peace.
  • Beginning of the Cold War between the USA and USSR.
  • Major shifts in global power, leading to decolonization.
