World War II

World War II was fought from 1939 to 1945, involving most of the world’s nations in two major alliances: the Allies and the Axis. It began when Germany invaded Poland, prompting Britain and France to declare war.

The war included major events such as the Battle of Britain, the Holocaust, the attack on Pearl Harbor, and the D-Day invasion. Millions of civilians and soldiers died worldwide.

The United States and Soviet Union played key roles in defeating the Axis Powers. Germany surrendered on May 8, 1945, and Japan surrendered on September 2, 1945, after the atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki.

Allies Victory: United States, Soviet Union, Britain, France, China, and others.
Axis Defeated: Germany, Italy, Japan.
