The Iran–Israel Conflict

The Iran–Israel conflict is a modern geopolitical struggle rooted in regional power, ideology, and security concerns. Tensions escalated after the 1979 Iranian Revolution, with Israel viewing Iran's nuclear ambitions and support for proxy groups as threats to its security.

The conflict has included proxy wars in Syria and Lebanon, missile development, and cyber operations. Both nations continue to monitor each other closely, and the situation remains a major concern for global security and the Middle East region.

Key Points

  • Iran supports groups like Hezbollah, Hamas, and others in the region.
  • Israel conducts military operations to protect its borders and security.
  • International diplomacy, sanctions, and negotiations are ongoing.
  • Regional stability is affected by this long-standing tension.
