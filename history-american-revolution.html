American Revolution

The American Revolution (1775–1783) was the struggle of the thirteen American colonies to gain independence from Britain. It started with the Battles of Lexington and Concord in 1775.

In 1776, the colonies adopted the Declaration of Independence. Major battles included Saratoga and Yorktown, with the latter leading to the surrender of British General Cornwallis.

The war officially ended with the Treaty of Paris in 1783, which recognized the United States as an independent nation.

Colonial Victory: United States of America
Defeated Power: Great Britain
