The Full Story of World War I

Real Life International History Series

The Beginning of the War

World War I began on July 28, 1914. The immediate cause of the war was the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria-Hungary in Sarajevo. He was killed by a Serbian nationalist named Gavrilo Princip.

Austria-Hungary blamed Serbia for the assassination and declared war. Because many European nations had formed military alliances, the conflict quickly spread across the continent.

The Two Sides

The war divided the world into two major alliances.

The Allied Powers included Britain, France, Russia, and later the United States and Italy.

The Central Powers included Germany, Austria-Hungary, the Ottoman Empire, and Bulgaria.

Life in the Trenches

Much of the war was fought in trenches across Europe, especially in France and Belgium. Soldiers lived in muddy trenches for months while facing constant enemy attacks.

New weapons made the war extremely deadly. These included machine guns, poison gas, tanks, and submarines.

The United States Enters the War

In 1917 the United States joined the war on the side of the Allies. American troops and resources strengthened the Allied forces and helped push back the Central Powers.

The End of the War

By 1918 Germany and its allies were exhausted. Food shortages, economic collapse, and military defeats weakened the Central Powers.

On November 11, 1918 Germany agreed to an armistice, ending the fighting.

The Treaty of Versailles

In 1919 the Treaty of Versailles officially ended the war. Germany was forced to accept responsibility for the conflict, reduce its military, and pay heavy reparations.

The Impact of the War

World War I caused more than 16 million deaths and led to the collapse of several powerful empires. The political changes that followed eventually helped lead to World War II twenty years later.
