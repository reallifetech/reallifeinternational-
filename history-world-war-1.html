World War I – The Great War

World War I, also called the Great War, began in 1914 after the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria-Hungary in Sarajevo. This single event triggered a chain reaction among the European powers, many of which had been building alliances and preparing for war for years.

The Central Powers, mainly Germany, Austria-Hungary, and the Ottoman Empire, faced the Allies, which included Britain, France, Russia, and later the United States. The war became global, affecting Europe, Africa, the Middle East, and Asia.

Trench warfare became a hallmark of World War I, leading to devastating casualties. New military technologies emerged, including tanks, machine guns, chemical weapons, and airplanes. Millions of soldiers and civilians lost their lives.

The war ended in 1918 with the signing of the Treaty of Versailles. Germany was forced to accept full responsibility, pay reparations, and cede territories. The map of Europe was redrawn, leading to political instability and laying the groundwork for World War II.

Key Outcomes

  • Over 16 million deaths and 20 million wounded.
  • Collapse of empires: Austro-Hungarian, Ottoman, German, and Russian.
  • Formation of the League of Nations to prevent future conflicts.
  • Global political and economic shifts that shaped the 20th century.
