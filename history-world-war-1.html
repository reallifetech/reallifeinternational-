World War I – The Great War

World War I (1914–1918), also known as the Great War, was one of the deadliest conflicts in history. It involved the major powers of Europe, divided into the Allies and the Central Powers, and led to unprecedented destruction and loss of life.

Causes

  • Nationalism: Intense national pride and rivalries among European nations increased tensions.
  • Imperialism: Competing empires sought colonies and resources, fueling conflicts abroad.
  • Militarism: Nations built up large armies and advanced weapons, preparing for war.
  • Alliance System: Europe was divided into two major alliances: the Allies and the Central Powers.
  • Assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand: The 1914 assassination in Sarajevo triggered the war.

Major Battles

The war was fought on multiple fronts, including:

  • Battle of the Marne: Stopped German advance into France.
  • Battle of Verdun: One of the longest and bloodiest battles in history.
  • Battle of the Somme: Massive casualties, symbolizing the horrors of trench warfare.

Outcome

The war ended in 1918 with the defeat of the Central Powers. The Treaty of Versailles imposed heavy reparations on Germany and redrew borders across Europe. Millions had died, and the political map of Europe was permanently altered.

Legacy

  • Set the stage for World War II due to political and economic instability.
  • Led to the formation of the League of Nations.
  • Transformed military technology and modern warfare tactics.
  • Left a lasting impact on global politics and society.
